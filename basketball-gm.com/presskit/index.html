Basketball GM is a basketball management simulation game. You don't play the games, you run the team. Signing players, making trades, figuring out when to tank so you can get a top pick in a good draft, etc. Kind of like "Association Mode" in NBA 2k or like the popular soccer game Football Manager.

Unique things about Basketball GM:

  1. It's free. Not "free, but it nags you to buy stuff in game". It's completely 100% free.
  2. Play in your web browser. No registration. No download. No nothing. Just go to https://play.basketball-gm.com/ and start playing.
  3. While other basketball sims exist, they kind of suck. And they don't have #1 and #2, which are completely unique to Basketball GM.

Current Userbase

2000 people per day. Average play time is 1 hour, so clearly some people are getting hooked. There is also an active user community on Reddit, including user-generated content like historical rosters, draft classes, etc.

Developer Info

Basketball GM is labor of love made almost entirely by one person: me. My name is Jeremy Scheff. I started writing Basketball GM in college many years ago as a way to learn some new programming stuff. I now work as a computational biologist, but I still spend a decent amount of time working on Basketball GM because it's a lot of fun.

I am always happy to discuss anything related to Basketball GM with anyone: jdscheff@gmail.com

Business Model

As mentioned above, the game is completely free. The only revenue I get are from some very unobtrusive ads. After you play for about 15 minutes, you might see an ad pop up over the game. This ad will distract you for about 5 seconds. After that, you won't see another ad until the following day, at the earliest. Surprisingly, this small amount of advertising nets me like $1000/month with my current userbase.

Everyone I ask for advice tells me to add some freemium/pay-to-win nonsense which would probably make a lot more money, but I don't want to do that.
